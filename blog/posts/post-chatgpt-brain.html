How to prevent brain degradation from ChatGPT

28 January 2023

I think I learned a lesson early in life that many people have experienced, but perhaps not noticed.

It goes something like this; when growing up in school and doing basic mathematics, your multiplication and division skills are sharp. Then as soon as you enter university and use a calculator for most basic mathematics, your brain gets slower with that activity.

The same effect can be said for writing essays. When applying to scholarships, I would often copy a lot of what I had written before and simply change a few words. This made my brain lazy and I never developed a strong skill for reasoning and quickly making good arguments for why I should get a scholarship.

Now with chatGPT, a lot of people can use it much like a calculator. You can enter a phrase and without thinking, let the AI think for you to come up with email prose or blog post. If my previous observations are true, then a lot of us will lose our writing and reasoning skills.

This lead me to the following corollary.

If it's inevitable that our brain power will atrophy given our tech aid and if you want to keep your brain power for whatever reason (maybe brain power is useless), then you really only have 2 options:

  1. Ensure that your daily work includes intellectual work that racks your brain. Be careful, because most work even if you're a programmer doesn't really rack your brain – an extreme example is using chatGPT to generate code for you.
  2. Set aside time every week where you deliberately train your brain, whether it's basic maths or writing essays.

Ultimately, you'll want to do your dayjob as efficiently as possible, so you really only have one option which is to set aside time for deliberate practice.

Maybe we don't need these skills that are being replaced?

"The strongest species is that which is able to adapt the fastest" - darwin (check)

That is afterall human's biggest strength. So another angle on this might be to leverage your calculator, copy-paste, and chatGPT as much as you can to buy yourself time for developing your brain in the areas that aren't automated yet.
